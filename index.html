The Art of Wine Tasting:

Tips for Beginners That Will Delight Your Senses

Wine tasting is a fascinating world. If you’re new to this exciting journey, don’t worry. We’re here to guide you through the art of wine tasting with 5 beginner-friendly tips. These essential basics will help you enjoy and appreciate wine like never before.

  1. Choose the Right Wine

    2. Before diving into tasting, it’s important to select the right wine. Our advice for beginners is to start with softer varieties. Remember that each grape has its own general characteristics, but these also depend on the region and the soil where it grows. Over time, you’ll be ready to explore more complex options.

  2. Observe the Color

    3. The first step in a good tasting is observing the color of the wine. Tilt the glass and look at its shades against a white background (a napkin or sheet of paper works well). This not only adds a touch of sophistication to your experience but also reveals details about the wine’s age and grape variety.

    For example, with a white grape like Viura, our young white wine shows a pale yellow color, while our Barrel-Fermented White, aged six months in oak, takes on a more golden hue due to oxidation. An easy way to picture this is the color of a freshly cut apple and how it turns brownish after a few hours. Generally, young red wines show a purplish color, while aged reds lean towards brick tones.

  3. Learn to Smell

    4. Aroma is a key part of wine tasting. First, smell the wine without swirling the glass. Then gently swirl it to release the aromas and take another sniff—notice the difference? Don’t be afraid to describe what you sense! Words like fruity, floral, or spicy are a great place to start.

  4. Taste and Savor

    5. When it’s time to taste the wine, take your time and pay attention to the sensations. Is it fruity, dry, or slightly acidic? Do you notice the tannins (that dry feeling on your gums)? Don’t worry if you can’t catch all the flavors right away; practice makes perfect.

  5. Pair with Simple Foods

    6. Enhance your tasting experience by pairing the wine with food. Try soft cheeses, fruits, or chocolates. This simple step can elevate the flavors and deepen your appreciation of the wine.
